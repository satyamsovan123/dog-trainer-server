Sit Part 1

‘Sit’ is the first obedience-type exercise that Shadow will learn. ‘Sit’ is the start of teaching him good manners, since it's such good greeting behaviour. If Shadow can 'sit' to greet someone, then he isn't jumping up which means no one is getting dirty paw marks, and you’re not getting funny looks. This lesson won't actually use the word 'Sit' yet, as that comes a little later!

You will need

Soft Treats

Steps

  1. Choose a time to train when Shadow is hungry; before a meal is a good idea. Also make sure that there are no distractions around to interfere (German Shepherd Dog puppies are often easily distracted!).

  2. Show Shadow you have a yummy treat in your hand and get him interested in it.

  3. Hold the treat by Shadow’s nose, and then lift your hand slowly up, and back towards his tail slightly.

  4. As his nose comes up, his bottom will go down. This probably won’t happen on the first go, so just keep trying. Yes, you may need a little extra patience!

  5. When Shadow sits, say your marker word (“Good”), and give him the treat immediately.

  6. Although you might be tempted, don’t push Shadow’s bottom down. This will make him feel wary about your hands and begin to worry about being touched.

  7. Repeat this until Shadow puts his bottom on the floor as soon as you move the treat up. There you have it!

Tips

  • If Shadow doesn’t immediately get the hang of this, don’t worry. That's perfectly normal; some puppies need several goes at this to start with. Especially German Shepherd Dogs!

  • If Shadow seems to lose his focus, let him wander off and try again in a few minutes. German Shepherd Dog puppies don’t tend to have an incredible attention span anyway.

  • Be patient! This is a new skill for you both that will soon be achieved. It just needs some time and patience.

  • Remember to say your marker word ("Good") the instant Shadow sits, and give the treat straight away as they do it. It will speed up the learning process quite significantly.
