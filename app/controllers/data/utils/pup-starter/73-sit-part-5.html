Sit Part 5

In this lesson, we’re going to focus on teaching Shadow to sit when you say “Sit” and removing their need to have a treat in view. To do this, Shadow needs to understand that a treat at your waist means they have to sit to get the treat. If you’re not sure Shadow is ready yet, just pop back to the library and have a read of previous lessons. Don’t worry if Shadow ever gets confused or doesn’t do something they did perfectly before. Just go back a step. Puppies have off-days, just like we do!

You will need:

  • Soft Treats
  • Treat pouch

Steps

  1. Repeat the last step you taught him so he can predict what you're going to do.
  2. Get Shadow interested in the treat and hold it close to your body. Wait until you see Shadow starting to sit. Say the word “sit” and as soon as he sits, say your reward word, and drop the treat.
  3. Repeat this 3 times.
  4. Now, do the same exercise, but without using the treat this time.
  5. When Shadow sits, say your reward word, and give a treat from your bag.
  6. Repeat this 3 times.
  7. When you are sure Shadow will sit, do almost everything the same. Gradually, start saying the word “sit” before he starts sitting.
  8. Repeat this 3 times, so that Shadow really starts to grasp this exercise.

Tips

  • Only say “Sit” once. Don’t get into the habit of repeating yourself because then Shadow will learn to ignore you until you've said something several times!
  • Make sure he understands each step before moving on to the next one.
  • It's good for you to know that this lesson involves lots of practice, so patience and persistence will be key!
  • Always give Shadow a treat when he gets it right to make the lesson sink in as much as possible.
  • Work at Shadow’s own speed. Some puppies get this very quickly, while others will take several sessions. Either way, it's all good, as you’ll be having fun together!
  • If everything is going well, gradually reduce the body language signals you are giving, until Shadow sits on the cue word alone.
