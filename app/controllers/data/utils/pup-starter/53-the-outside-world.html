The outside world

It's now time to show Shadow a little more of the outside world so that he can experience all the sounds, smells and sights that he will need to get used to. Remember to take this at Shadow's own pace - if he is less confident, take this slowly.

Let's begin!

Choose a time of day when it is relatively busy outside and have at least 20 treats in your pocket. Carry Shadow to the end of your garden or driveway and let him watch whatever is going on from a safe distance, e.g. people, other dogs, traffic.

With Shadow in your arms, go for a short walk with him so he can experience all these new sights, sounds and scents. Feel free to enjoy your walk for as long as Shadow is happy or your arms can manage it!

Give him the occasional treat, about one every 30 seconds. This will make the experience even more positive!

Stop and talk to people whenever you can - it will be a great learning experience for him.

Ask people not to touch Shadow yet. Because you are holding him, he can't move away if he feels uncomfortable.

Remember Shadow might not be fully vaccinated, so don't put him on the ground just yet if that’s the case. If Shadow seems at all worried by anything, move further away. Don’t take him any closer to 'see that it's safe' because this could set up fears in the future - and we don't want those!
