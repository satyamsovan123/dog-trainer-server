Meeting Another Dog Outdoors

Of course, we want Shadow to be friendly with people and other dog friends. You have nothing to worry about for now - German Shepherd Dogs are very social dogs and are quite fond of playing with others.

You will need:

  • Soft Treats
  • Toys
  • 2m Training Lead
  • Harness
  • Another dog friend

Steps

  1. Ask your friend to bring their dog into the garden.
  2. The dog must be on a lead and should be fully vaccinated.
  3. Then, bring Shadow out into the garden, and sit quietly together with your friend and their dog.
  4. Let Shadow lead the interaction. At this point, you won’t know how he may react. He might start off a little worried or be wildly excited at the thought of a new friend. Either is fine!
  5. It's better if we lay off a little here and not encourage Shadow to interact with the older dog in any way, so that he gets to make the choice for himself.
  6. Giving both dogs treats at the same time is a good idea - try to do this about 5 times during the visit.

Tips

  • We know it might be really tempting, but let's not go on to play off-lead games for now. That will come later!
  • Make sure Shadow is the one to choose how to approach and interact with new friends. This will help him feel more confident and in control.
  • Keeping Shadow's attention around other dogs will certainly be a challenge! But nothing you can't manage.
  • You’ll have time to work more on this in the coming weeks.
