Biting and Sleep

You now know why puppies bite and how to deflect biting onto toys, but there are other things to be aware of that can also increase play biting and make it worse.

You will need:

  • Stuffed chew toy
  • Crate or Playpen
  • Licky Mat

Steps

  1. Puppies need a lot of sleep – and at this age, Shadow should be sleeping for around 18 hours a day.
  2. Make a timetable of Shadow's regular daily routine and see if he is getting enough sleep. Many ‘bitey’ puppies are actually over-tired. This often happens when the whole family is at home, as there is always something going on, and that can prevent Shadow from getting enough good quality sleep.
  3. If you discover that Shadow isn’t getting the sleep he needs, make sure you schedule that into his day.
  4. You can use the crate or playpen, so they can sleep close to you whatever you are doing – but not be tempted to join in.
  5. Give them a stuffed chew toy or lickimat to help settle them down into that much-needed nap.

Tips

  • Have a game or a training session first, so you know that Shadow is tired and more likely to sleep.
  • Make sure Shadow has been to the toilet, so they are likely to settle and sleep.
  • Remember that puppies sleep in lots of short naps during the day. Make sure all the family know that when Shadow is sleeping, he isn’t to be disturbed.
