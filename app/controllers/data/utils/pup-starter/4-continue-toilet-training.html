Continue toilet training

What a great start you've made on your training! From now on, each day (and night!) will get easier as you and Shadow get to know each other more, and he starts to understand what you want from him. For example, how having a wee on your new carpet is not quite acceptable.

If Shadow hasn't got it quite yet, there's no need to worry. Toilet training for German Shepherd Dog puppies takes a lot of time and patience from you, and also requires for Shadow to not only have to pick up on the lesson, but he to develop more bladder and bowel control. Having said this, you can probably see how toilet training isn't likely to happen overnight - but you can be sure that time, development, and repetition will guide you through it.

Here's how to keep winning at the toilet training game:

  • Carry on doing exactly the same as you started yesterday.
  • Learn Shadow's own tell-tale signals that he needs to go to the toilet (such as sniffing or circling) and take him to the toilet as soon as you pick up on them. Every puppy is different though, so try to pay extra attention to understand your own.
  • During the day, remember to watch him closely so he doesn't get a chance to make a mistake, but rather an opportunity to get it right! Remember that every success you can reward (and the fewer mistakes you let Shadow make) the faster he will be toilet trained.

Tips

  • Start by taking Shadow to the bathroom more than you think you need to. Trust us, it's better that you have a wasted trip outside or to your toilet area than he makes a mistake in the house where you don't want him to go.
  • Remember to reward every single success so that the message stays clear with them, and you can move along toilet training faster.
  • Some days, you'll feel like you're just not getting anywhere with Shadow's toilet training. Don't worry - you definitely are! Every day is a chance to improve. You just need some time, consistency, and repetition.
