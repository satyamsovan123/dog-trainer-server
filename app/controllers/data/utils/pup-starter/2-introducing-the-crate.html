Introducing the crate

A crate (or a small puppy pen) will be a lifesaver in Shadow’s toilet and sleep training. Make sure you introduce it properly to him, so he comes to see it as a comfortable and safe place to be in. Don’t worry if it takes a while for Shadow to get used to the crate, he will come around sooner than you know.

You will need

  • Soft Treats
  • Toys
  • Crate or Playpen

Steps

  1. Introduce Shadow to the crate, keeping the door open.
  2. Encourage him to go into the crate, possibly by chasing a toy, or a tasty titbit. Never force him in though! We promise you, this won’t work.
  3. Don’t shut the door! Use this time to just let him feel comfortable going inside at his own pace.
  4. A good tip is to feed Shadow his meals in the crate, and stay beside him to keep them company!
  5. Whenever Shadow is sleepy, put him in the crate beside you. This way he gets used to going in his cozy den every time he wants to sleep.
  6. Remember to keep the door open during this introductory phase.

Tips

  • Put a few treats and toys into the crate, it’s a good way to get Shadow to investigate it.
  • It's not a good idea to disturb Shadow when he is in the crate. Make this a hard and fast rule with everyone in the family. The crate is going to be Shadow’s sanctuary, so it’s important that it feels that way to him.
  • Having 2 crates will make your life easier. One in your living room, and one in the bedroom should do the trick.
  • Remember to keep the door open during this introductory phase.
