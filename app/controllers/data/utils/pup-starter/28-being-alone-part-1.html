Teaching Independence to Shadow

Teaching Shadow that he can’t always follow you around is an important first step towards him being independent and feeling okay when left alone. To make the transition smoother, you'll have to start practicing this from the very beginning of your life together. Bear in mind that teaching will require time, and lots of patience as being left alone is not the most natural for any dog, including German Shepherd Dogs (or humans, for that matter!). Shadow loves to be around you - you are his family and he feels safe with you.

You will need:

  • Baby Gate
  • Puppy's meal

Steps

  1. Give Shadow his meal at the usual time.
  2. While he is eating, start by quietly leaving the room for 1 minute only.
  3. Use a baby gate on the doorway to wherever you feed Shadow. This way he can see where you’ve gone and not worry about it.

Tips

  • Most German Shepherd Dogs are so focused on their food, they will hardly even notice you’ve gone. This is a good thing!
  • If Shadow looks at all worried, only go as far as the other side of the baby gate for the first few times.
  • No need to stay away too long - just 1 minute in this first week will do!
