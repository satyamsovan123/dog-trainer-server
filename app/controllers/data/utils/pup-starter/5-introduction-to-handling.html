Introduction to handling

Remember that Shadow needs time and patience to adjust to his new home and family. A calm approach will always be the right way to go - it simply builds trust and comfort! Many dogs are worried about being handled, or grow up scared of people’s hands, sadly... this is often because they have been grabbed or handled roughly, so make sure you start in the right way from the very beginning.

You will need

Get comfy!

Steps:

  1. First, sit on the floor.
  2. For now, don’t hold Shadow or make him stay with you. Just let him investigate you at his own pace.
  3. If he walks off, don’t worry, it doesn’t mean you’re boring. Let him do his thing, and wait until he comes back.
  4. When he does, let him smell your hands. Once he is confident, you can start to stroke him down the sides of his neck and shoulders. Maybe even do a gentle bottom scratch!
  5. Observe Shadow and work out what places he enjoys being touched. Stay away from sensitive bits like the face and paws, for now.

Tips

  • Talk to Shadow in a gentle, quiet voice so he gets used to it.
  • Spend as long as you want doing this - every minute is building Shadow’s confidence anyway.
  • Look out for Shadow starting to get really excited. If this happens, just have a quick break, but don’t tell him off or push him away.
