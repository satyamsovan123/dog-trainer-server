A new home

Now that Shadow is getting familiar with his new home, it's time for him to explore a bit more.

Here's a few tips for how to help him get there:

  • Make sure the rooms Shadow is going to have access to are safe. This means tidying up, and getting rid of wires, poisonous plants, or other hazards that can get him into a pickle. Remember that German Shepherd Dog puppies investigate things with their mouths, not just eyes and noses!
  • If you have a garden, ensure it has secure fencing and look for potential escape routes and hazards!
  • Decide what rooms Shadow is going to be allowed into. This might be the whole house, or you may want certain rooms to be off-limits. These can be closed off with baby gates.
  • Let Shadow explore the house in his own time under your supervision. Never push him into investigating anything. If he isn't too keen on something - this should all be done at his own speed.
  • In each room (and garden), just sit quietly for a couple of minutes. Let Shadow get used to his new home and allow him to explore the outdoor space, and experience the sights, sounds, and scents of his new area. You can spend as long as you want doing this really; every minute is building Shadow's confidence.

Talk to him all the time in a gentle quiet voice to help give him even more of a confidence boost. This also helps in building the bond between you.
