Teaching 'Leave It'

Puppies are curious and like to check everything out. That’s how they learn best, after all. However, there’s some things you’d rather they didn’t get involved with and times when you wish they’d leave things alone and come away from them.

‘Leave it’ is a really good exercise of self-control, as it also helps your puppy to work through their frustration. There's a lot of problem-solving skills going on here as they work out what gets them the treats.

It’s a great cue to have in your vocabulary for when you feel like you want to say ‘no’ because it just works in the same way, but instead of sounding negative, it gives your puppy a better choice to make: ‘leave that and do this instead’.

Essentially, ‘leave it’ works to interrupt a behavior you don’t want. It could be something like this: ‘Leave that dog you’re staring at’, and teach them to disengage or look away, or ‘leave that pile of fox poo’ and come here instead.

Let’s get prepared

  • Treat bag
  • Some of their dry kibble or treats

Tips

  • Don’t rush this one! Patience now will pay off in dividends later.
  • We don’t want them to ever take the food from your hand or the floor, so go slowly and methodically so they learn that ‘leaving it’ is a good thing to do.
