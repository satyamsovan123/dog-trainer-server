Toilet Training Introduction

Just to manage your expectations, there is no way of getting toilet training right in one go. But precisely because we know how tricky it can be, we've done our best at breaking down the training into easy steps with explanations to follow. Oh, and remember that Shadow is just a puppy...so don't expect him to be fully toilet trained until around 16 weeks old.

The first thing you need to know is that there are two parts to toilet training your German Shepherd Dog puppy:

  1. Toilet training during the day (with and without a garden)
  2. Toilet training during the night

Both are slightly different, but there is only one key thing that will help you succeed - being watchful!

Toilet training by day if you have a garden

Here are some simple steps for you to follow:

  • Use baby gates in the doorways of the main room you and Shadow spend most of your day in so he stays close to you and don't let him out of your sight! You'll need to keep an eye on him all the time. And yes - we do mean all of it!
  • While you are establishing Shadow's routine, you'll need to take him out every hour for a trip to the loo. Be prepared to wait! German Shepherd Dog puppies can easily get distracted, no matter how much they may need to go!
  • Always have at least 6 treats in your pocket and always be ready to reward toilet success.
  • Reward Shadow with a treat as soon as he goes to the toilet in his designated place.
  • Keep track of the times he needs to go out. These will mostly be when he wakes up, after or during a game, after a meal, and after a drink - but keep an eye out for other times, maybe Shadow has a special time in the day he prefers.
  • Learn Shadow's signs for when he needs to go to the toilet. These can be sniffing the floor, turning in circles, trying to find a corner, whining, or just being restless. If you see any of the signs, take him outside as quickly as possible!
  • Always go with Shadow so you can keep track of whether he has gone to the toilet so that you're able to reward him. This is key for showing him he has done a good thing!

Toilet training by day if you don't have a garden

First off - it's important to designate a toilet area for Shadow. This might be in several places depending on your home set up. Some examples could be:

  • Your roof terrace
  • Your balcony
  • Your bathroom
  • The corner of your lounge

Wherever it is, the area needs to be easily cleanable. Think about putting down a waterproof membrane if you have carpets/wood or have the toilet area on tiled floors. You’ll thank us later! Some people like to use puppy pads or newspaper, some people prefer fake grass, and others are happy for their puppy to pee onto the concrete and rinse it off. You can also look at building a dirt box or putting some turf inside a tray to use as a puppy toilet. It’s a good idea to check with the breeder if Shadow has been using a particular surface for the toilet – they’ll feel more comfortable going to the toilet at yours if they have the same surface to go on. Once you are all set up, you are now ready to get Shadow started.

  • Use baby gates in the doorways of the main room you and Shadow spend most of your day in so he stays close to you and don't let him out of your sight! You'll need to keep an eye on him all the time. And yes - we do mean all of it!
  • While you are establishing Shadow's routine, you'll need to take him to the toilet area every hour for a trip to the loo. Be prepared to wait! German Shepherd Dog puppies can easily get distracted, no matter how much they may need to go!
  • Always have at least 6 treats in your pocket and always be ready to reward toilet success.
  • Reward Shadow with a treat as soon as he goes to the toilet in his designated place.
  • Keep track of the times he needs to go out. These will mostly be when he wakes up, after or during a game, after a meal, and after a drink - but keep an eye out for other times, maybe Shadow has a special time in the day he prefers.
  • Learn Shadow's signs for when he needs to go to the toilet. These can be sniffing the floor, turning in circles, trying to find a corner, whining, or just being restless. If you see any of the signs, take him to their toilet spot as quickly as possible!

Toilet training by night

When you're toilet training at night, your routine will be quite different than in the daytime. Here is how to get on the right track:

  • Try to go to bed quite late for your first few nights. The later you go, the more Shadow will be able to sleep through the night too.
  • Make sure that the last thing you do before you go to bed is to take Shadow to the toilet! Oh, and be prepared to wait as long as it takes for them to go. Might be a while! German Shepherd Dog puppies get easily distracted and so a lot of toilet training is about patience...on your part, of course.
  • Here comes the fun part...you'll have to set the alarm twice in the middle of the night (at around 2.30am and 4.30am) so that he is able to go in the right spot when he needs to, and you get to wake up with no accidents beside you. Everybody wins!
  • Make sure you go with Shadow so you can reward any successes, and so you know for sure if he has gone to the toilet or not.
  • If he wakes up at any other time and really won't settle, this probably means he needs to go to the toilet again (a puppy won't sleep if they need the toilet!).
  • In the morning, aim to get up early (at around 7:00 am), and take him to the toilet straight away before you do anything else. Believe us, Shadow is highly likely to want to go to the toilet then!

Tips

  • Remember that the golden secret to successful daytime toilet training for German Shepherd Dog puppies is to keep your eyes open, so they won't get a chance to make a mistake.
  • The most important piece of advice we can give you is to always reward Shadow when they go to the toilet in the right place. Yep, every single time. This will let him know you are really happy with what he has done, which will make toilet training happen much faster.
  • Never punish mistakes! Big no-no. This will make your toilet training take longer, may cause behavior problems, and risks breaking down your relationship with Shadow, so it’s best to avoid that. Simply be as watchful as you can to prevent accidents.
  • Clean up accidents in places where they shouldn't be, with an enzyme-based (not ammonia-based) cleaner to prevent him from wanting to go to the loo there again.

Don't worry, as Shadow develops physically, he will be able to hold on for longer. Then you can gradually cut out one of the 'middle of the night' toilet visits until you are only getting up once. Glory days ahead.
