Grooming Part 1

German Shepherd Dogs love the great outdoors so much that they often bring some of it back home with them in their coat. This means you might be spending quite a lot of time grooming! Don't worry, we'll make sure this is a good experience for you both.

You will need:

  • Soft Treats
  • Soft Brush

Steps

  1. Give Shadow a chance to see and sniff the brush, so he knows it’s nothing to worry about.
  2. Stroke Shadow gently with your hand at first, and then run the brush down the side of his neck and shoulders.
  3. Stay away from sensitive areas like his paws, stomach, and face for now.
  4. After each few brush strokes, give Shadow a treat.

Tips

  • Make sure the brush is soft enough that it is comfortable for Shadow, but still stiff enough to get through stubborn knots in his coat.
  • It’s best to do this while Shadow is relaxed beside you or on your lap. Let's not make a big thing of it or try to restrain him as you want him to feel happy, relaxed, and make grooming and handling a positive experience overall.
  • Make sure everyone in the family knows to handle and interact with Shadow gently!
  • Try getting him used to you running your hands down his more sensitive areas after he is comfortable being brushed elsewhere. Gotta get to those spots someday!
